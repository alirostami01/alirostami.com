Benefits of Using Obsidian

Ali Rostami
 2023-04-13  780 words   4 minutes 
Contents

Obsidian is a note-taking app that has been gaining popularity among knowledge workers, researchers, and writers. It is based on the idea of a zettelkasten, a note-taking system popularized by the German sociologist Niklas Luhmann. Here are some of the benefits of using Obsidian and a zettelkasten:

  1. Better organization: With Obsidian, you can organize your notes using links, tags, and folders. This makes it easy to find information when you need it, and it helps you avoid the chaos of having notes scattered across multiple platforms.

  2. Better retention: Zettelkasten is designed to help you remember more of what you learn. The idea is that you take notes on everything you read, watch, or hear, and then connect those notes to other notes that are related. This helps you form a web of knowledge that you can easily navigate and recall.

  3. More creativity: By connecting your notes in new and unexpected ways, you can spark your creativity and come up with new ideas. Obsidian’s graph view makes it easy to see the connections between your notes, which can help you make new connections and see patterns you might not have noticed before.

  4. More productivity: Obsidian’s keyboard shortcuts and Markdown support make it easy to create and edit notes quickly. And because you can find information more easily, you can spend less time searching for what you need and more time working on your projects.

  5. More collaboration: Obsidian allows you to share your notes with others, either through a shared folder or by publishing your notes as a website. This makes it easy to collaborate on projects or share your knowledge with others.

Using Obsidian and a zettelkasten can help you organize your notes, retain more of what you learn, spark your creativity, be more productive, and collaborate more effectively. If you’re looking for a better way to manage your notes and ideas, Obsidian and a zettelkasten might be just what you need.

Sync your Obsidian files with Syncthing

Syncthing is a great choice for syncing files between devices, and it works seamlessly with Obsidian. Here are some benefits of using Syncthing to sync your Obsidian files:

  1. Seamless syncing: Syncthing can be configured to sync your Obsidian files automatically between your Android and Linux devices. This means that you can work on your notes on any device and they will always be up to date.

  2. Secure syncing: Syncthing uses encryption to secure your files as they are synced between devices. This ensures that your notes and other sensitive information are protected from unauthorized access.

  3. Easy to use: Syncthing is easy to set up and use, with a user-friendly interface that makes it easy to configure syncing options and monitor the status of your sync.

  4. Highly customizable: Syncthing offers a wide range of customizable options that allow you to fine-tune the way your files are synced. You can choose which folders to sync, set up advanced syncing options, and more.

  5. Open source software: Syncthing is open source software, which means that the code is freely available for anyone to view and modify. This ensures that the software is transparent and secure.

Here are the steps to set up Syncthing to sync your Obsidian files between your Android and Linux devices:

  1. Install Syncthing: First, you’ll need to download and install Syncthing on both your Android and Linux devices. You can find the download links for each platform on the Syncthing website.

  2. Configure Syncthing: Once Syncthing is installed on both devices, you’ll need to configure it to sync your Obsidian files. Here’s how:

  • On your Android device, open the Syncthing app and tap the “Add Device” button. Scan the QR code that appears on your Linux device’s Syncthing interface, or enter the Device ID manually.
  • On your Linux device, click the “Add Remote Device” button and enter the Device ID from your Android device’s Syncthing interface.
  • On both devices, select the folder(s) you want to sync. In this case, you’ll want to select the folder where your Obsidian notes are stored. Make sure that the folder path is the same on both devices.
  • Choose the syncing options that best fit your needs. For example, you can choose to sync continuously, or only when both devices are on the same Wi-Fi network.
  1. Monitor the syncing status: Once you’ve configured Syncthing, you can monitor the syncing status from either device’s interface. You’ll see a list of all the folders being synced, along with their current status.

And that’s it! With Syncthing set up, your Obsidian notes will be automatically synced between your Android and Linux devices. This makes it easy to work on your notes from anywhere, and ensures that your notes are always up to date.
